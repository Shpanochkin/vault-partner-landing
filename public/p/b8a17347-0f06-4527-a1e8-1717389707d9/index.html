The partnership model
Built around your success,
not our fees.
Pass-through at cost
Every supplier cost — KYC, card issuance, settlement, infrastructure — is billed to you at our actual cost, with zero markup. You always see exactly what we pay.
Net income, shared
Vault calculates net income: total end-user fee revenue minus supplier costs. That net income is shared with you according to the tiers below. You receive the majority.
We earn nothing else
No setup fee. No platform fee. No management fee. Our only income is the portion of net revenue we retain after paying your share. Fully aligned.
Revenue share tiers
Two dimensions that grow with you

Your economics improve on two independent axes: your share of net income scales with active users, and Vault's interchange share scales with card volume.

1. Net income share — by monthly active users
Net Income = End-User Fee Revenue − Supplier Costs Your Payout = Net Income × Your Share % Evaluated monthly on active user count.
Monthly Active Users Your Share
0 – 1,999 50%
2,000 – 9,999 60%
10,000 – 49,999 70%
50,000+ 75%

At 50,000+ active users you reach the top tier: 75% of net income to you. Vault retains only 25%.

2. Interchange income — included in the revenue pool
Interchange Income = Card TPV × Rebate Rate (1.20%) Card networks (Visa/MC) pay interchange on every card transaction. Vault receives a contractual rebate of 1.00–1.50% of card volume from the BIN sponsor, depending on total platform volume. This income flows into the shared revenue pool and is split according to the net income tiers above. No separate per-client interchange tier — one pool, one split.
